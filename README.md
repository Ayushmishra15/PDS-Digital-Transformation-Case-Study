## Overview
This is an independent consulting case study analysing 
the end-to-end digital transformation of India's Public 
Distribution System (PDS) — the world's largest food 
security programme serving over 80 crore beneficiaries 
across 36 states and union territories.

The study follows a structured consulting methodology 
covering problem identification, transformation framework, 
stakeholder analysis, impact assessment, risk management 
and strategic recommendations — modelled on how top 
consulting firms like EY approach government transformation 
engagements.

---

## Problem Statement
Before digitisation, India's PDS was severely broken:
- 5.8 crore fake and ghost ration cards were active
- 40–50% subsidy leakage in many states
- Beneficiaries locked to single Fair Price Shop
- No real-time supply chain visibility
- Zero government oversight or audit trail

---

## The Digital Transformation
Four key technology interventions transformed the system:

| Intervention | What It Did |
|---|---|
| Aadhaar Integration | Biometric authentication at point of collection |
| ePoS Devices | 5.33 lakh machines deployed at all ration shops |
| ONORC Scheme | Collect rations from any FPS across 36 states |
| Digital Supply Chain | End-to-end tracking from warehouse to last mile |

## ONORC Rollout Highlight
ONORC grew from 4 states covering 19% of India's food 
security population in August 2019 to all 36 states 
covering 100% by June 2022 — a complete national rollout 
in under 3 years despite COVID-19 disruptions.
